What is a fable?

A fable is a brief traditional story that uses memorable characters and events to explore a choice, habit, or consequence. Its lesson is an invitation to reflect, not a rule that ends discussion.

Frequently asked questions

What is the moral of The Tortoise and the Hare?

A common moral is that steady effort may outperform careless talent.

Is The Tortoise and the Hare in the public domain?

The traditional story is public domain; this site’s original retelling and artwork are new.

Did Aesop write the exact version on this website?

No. This is an original retelling of a traditional fable commonly attributed to Aesop.

Can the fable have more than one lesson?

Yes. Readers may also reflect on pride, attention, and underestimating others.

Full story and transcript

Hare was fast and proud of it. He teased Tortoise until Tortoise invited him to race. Hare sprinted ahead, but stopped to rest beneath a tree. Tortoise continued one steady step at a time, passed the sleeping hare, and reached the finish first.

Child-view transcript

Hare boasted, Tortoise challenged, and the race began. Hare slept while Tortoise kept going. The finish belonged to the patient racer.

Moral

Steady effort can succeed when talent becomes careless.

Discuss together

When does careful practice help? How can confidence remain kind? What small step can you take today?

About this experience

The Tortoise and the Hare is a traditional fable commonly attributed to Aesop. The underlying story is in the public domain. This website uses an original retelling and newly created SVG illustrations.

The visual direction draws from decorative techniques found in late-19th-century children’s books, including public-domain work by Walter Crane. Walter Crane did not create these new illustrations.

Narration is produced using a speech-synthesis voice provided by the visitor’s browser or operating system. Voice availability and quality vary by device.
